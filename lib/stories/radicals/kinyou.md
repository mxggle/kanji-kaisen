## Origin

The kinyou radical is a pictograph of a low, sturdy wooden table or a simple stool used for sitting or placing objects. It represents the supporting surfaces that make human domestic life possible.

## Story

Take a seat or set it down! This radical represents the quiet, functional surfaces of the home—the tables and benches that support our daily work and rest. When you see it, think of structural support, stability, and the physical foundation of an object. It brings a sense of grounded utility and the simple comfort of household furniture to any character it joins.

## Position

Often wraps around the bottom or appears as a central component in complex kanji.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates furniture (tables, stools), physical support and stability, specific locations, or the concept of things being "ordinary" and common.

## Example Kanji

### 処 (deal with / treat / place / manage)

Walking (夂) + table/stool (几). Originally referred to walking to a specific "table" or "place" to "deal with" a matter or manage an affair.

### 机 (desk / table)

Wood (木) + table (几). A functional supporting "table" crafted from "wood"—the literal definition of a modern or traditional desk.

### 凡 (ordinary / mediocre / general)

Table (几) + a central mark. Something that is as common and uncomplicated as a simple "stool"—representing the "ordinary" or general nature of things.
