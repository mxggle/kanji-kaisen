## Origin

The gandare radical is a pictograph of a steep mountain cliff or a rocky overhang. It suggests a place of height, a natural barrier, or a sheltered space carved into the side of a mountain.

## Story

Watch your step! This radical represents the sheer, unyielding wall of a mountain. When you see this "overhang" above a character, think of the height of the peaks, the solidity of stone, and the natural protection found beneath an outcropping. it brings the weight and enduring stability of the earth to its characters.

## Position

Always appears at the TOP and LEFT side, forming an "enclosure" or "cliff" shape.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates geological features (cliffs, stones), height, massive weight/pressure, or buildings and shelters built into rocky terrain.

## Example Kanji

### 原 (original / field / source)

Cliff (厂) + spring (泉). A place where water springs forth directly from a "cliff" side—the "original" and pure source of a great river.

### 厚 (thick / kind / substantial)

Cliff (厂) + child/sun (子/日). Imagine a sun rising over a "thick," massive wall of stone, suggesting depth, substance, and a kind, heavy heart.

### 圧 (pressure / push)

Cliff (厂) + earth (土). The immense weight of a massive "cliff" pressing down upon the "earth"—the literal representation of force and pressure.
