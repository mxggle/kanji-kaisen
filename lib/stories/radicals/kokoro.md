## Origin

Originally a pictograph of the human heart, showing its chambers and the vessels carrying blood. Over time, it came to represent both the physical organ and the intangible soul, mind, and emotions.

## Story

The ancient character once looked like a beating heart with its arteries. Today, 心 represents the very center of who we are—the place where our feelings bloom, our thoughts grow, and our spirit resides. It is the core of human emotion and consciousness.

## Position

Appears at the bottom of a kanji, or as the vertical side radical (risshinben) on the left

## Transforms Meaning

Relates to emotions, psychological states, thoughts, feelings, or the deep inner spirit of a person

## Example Kanji

### 思 (think)

A field/brain (田) over a heart (心) = the mind and heart working together in harmony to produce thoughts.

### 愛 (love)

A person (the top parts) shielding and wrapping their heart (心) around another; the profound feeling of care and affection.

### 忘 (forget)

To perish or disappear (亡) + heart (心) = when an image or a memory fades away and 'dies' out of the heart's reaches.
