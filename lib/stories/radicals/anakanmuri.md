## Origin

The radical form of 穴 (hole) when it sits as a crown. It depicts a roof over an opening, signifying a space within or beneath.

## Story

When you see this 'hole' crown on top of a kanji, imagine a roof with an opening. It tells you that the character involves a hollow space, a window, or something that is hidden deep inside a structure.

## Position

Always appears at the TOP of the kanji

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates hollow spaces, openings, or the quality of being deep and cavernous

## Example Kanji

### 窓 (window)

Hole radical + spirit/heart = the opening in the wall where the house 'breathes' and looks out.

### 窃 (steal / stealth)

Hole radical + cut (切) = a thief cutting their way through a hole in the wall to sneak inside.

### 窯 (kiln / oven)

Hole radical + fire/potter = a large cavernous oven used to bake pottery and bread.
