## Origin

The kai radical is a pictograph of a cowry shell, which was used as the primary form of currency in ancient China. The character clearly shows the shell's distinctive markings and the valuable, shimmering interior.

## Story

Treasure the moment! This radical represents the very origins of commerce and the primal concept of wealth. When you see it, think of money, shimmering shells, precious objects, and the things we value most in life. It brings the spirit of the ancient marketplace and the weight of genuine treasure to all of its characters, reminding us of what is truly worth keeping.

## Position

Often appears as a standalone kanji or as the bottom component in complex characters.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates money, wealth, precious items, buying/selling, prizes, or the value and cost associated with an activity.

## Example Kanji

### 貝 (shell / seashell)

The base kanji for a cowry shell, once the most important and widespread currency in the ancient world.

### 買 (buy / purchase)

Network/enclosure (罒) + shell (貝). To bring a valuable "shell" into your "enclosure" or hand—the literal action of buying something.

### 費 (expense / cost / spend)

Shell/money (貝) + split/negative (弗). The precious "money" or "shells" that are "split" away from you when paying—representing your total expenses.
