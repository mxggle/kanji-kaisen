## Origin

A pictograph of a recurve bow used for archery. The shape shows the bow bent and held under tension, ready to release its power through an arrow.

## Story

See the curve of a great bow held steady by a master archer! This character represents the beauty of tension and the potential of energy waiting to be released. It is the root of kanji related to archery, stretching, and anything that bends without breaking.

## Position

Standalone kanji or on the left side (yumihen)

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates bows, archery, the act of bending or stretching, or the state of being under tension

## Example Kanji

### 引 (pull / draw)

Bow radical + a single line = the act of drawing back the string of a bow before releasing the arrow.

### 弘 (vast / wide)

Bow radical + personal/self = the vast, wide arc of a powerful bow stretching out toward the horizon.

### 強 (strong)

Bow radical + insect/creature (mushi) = the tremendous physical strength required to string and draw a bow made of animal horn and sinew.
