## Origin

The kane radical is a pictograph of a smelting furnace with nuggets of precious metal or "gold" being extracted from the earth. It represents the immense value, utility, and refined beauty of metallic materials across human history.

## Story

Dig deep for treasure! This radical represents the metals that form the backbone of our technology and the "gold" that has served as a global standard of value for centuries. When you see it, think of industrial strength, immense wealth, rare minerals, and the enduring tools forged from the earth's riches. It brings a sense of absolute value, durability, and a shining, timeless brilliance to any character it joins.

## Position

Often appears as a standalone kanji or at the bottom of complex characters.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates metals (gold, silver, iron), money and finances, minerals, physical value and worth, or things that are hard and shiny.

## Example Kanji

### 金 (gold / metal / money / Friday)

The base kanji for precious metal and the fundamental unit of human wealth (money), also representing the "metal" day of the week.

### 釜 (pot / cauldron / kettle)

Gold/metal (金) + father (父). A sturdy, heavy metal "pot" used by the head of a prosperous household to feed a large family gathering.

### 鑑 (specimen / mirror / discern / model)

Gold/metal (金) + observe (監). Originally referred to a polished "metal" bowl filled with water used as a "mirror" to observe oneself and discern the clear truth.
