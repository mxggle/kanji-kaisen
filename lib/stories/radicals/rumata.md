## Origin

The rumata radical is a pictograph of a hand (又) holding a heavy weapon, specifically a spiked lance or a cudgel. It represents the forceful application of power and the active process of striking, guiding, or transforming something through physical effort.

## Story

Take action! Imagine a hand gripping a sturdy wooden shaft, ready to strike or move an object with force. This radical is the soul of effort and physical change. When you see it on the right side, think of a strong action, a strike, or the heavy "shaft" of a tool being used to accomplish a grand task.

## Position

Always appears on the RIGHT side of the kanji.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates physical force, striking, guidance, the use of handheld tools or weapons, or the active "doing" of a complex process.

## Example Kanji

### 殿 (palace / lord)

Enclosure/residency + lance shaft (殳). Originally referred to a guarded "palace" where warriors with "lances" stood watch to protect their "lord."

### 殺 (kill / murder)

Hand/tool + lance shaft (殳). A violent "action" taken with a "lance" or weapon—the literal representation of the act of killing.

### 殻 (shell / hull)

Vessel/pot + lance shaft (殳). The act of "striking" a vessel or grain with a "shaft" to remove the outer "shell" or hull.
