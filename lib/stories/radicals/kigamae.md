## Origin

The kigamae radical is a pictograph of rising clouds of steam or the invisible currents of the moving air. It represents the vital life force (qi/ki) that flows through all living things and the atmosphere.

## Story

Take a deep breath! This radical represents the invisible energy of the air and the "ki" that moves within your own body. When you see it wrap around the top of a character, think of weather patterns, human emotions (the spirit), and the gaseous states of matter. It brings a sense of atmosphere, vital energy, and the powerful, invisible forces of nature to its characters.

## Position

Wraps around the top and right side of the kanji.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates the air, gases (steam, vapor), the spirit or energy (ki), emotions, and atmospheric phenomena (weather).

## Example Kanji

### 気 (spirit / energy / air / mood)

Steam (气) + rice (メ/米). Imagine the nutrient-rich "steam" rising from a bowl of hot "rice"—it represents the essential energy or "spirit" that fuels life.

### 汽 (steam / vapor)

Water (氵) + steam (气). The physical "water" that has been transformed into rising "vapor" through the application of intense heat.

### 氛 (atmosphere / aura / mood)

Steam (气) + share/divide (分). The "air" or "spirit" that surrounds a person or location, "dividing" and defining the local "aura" or mood.
