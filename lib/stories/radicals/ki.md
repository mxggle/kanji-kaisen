## Origin

A pictograph of a tree with a strong trunk (vertical line), branches reaching out (top horizontal), and roots spreading into the soil (bottom strokes).

## Story

Stand before a great, ancient tree and see 木! The vertical line is the sturdy trunk, the top strokes are branches reaching for the sunlight, and the bottom strokes are the roots gripping the earth. It is the root of all things natural and the source of our wooden world.

## Position

Standalone kanji, on the left side (kihen), or occasionally at the bottom

## Transforms Meaning

Relates to trees, wood, forests, or objects and tools traditionally made from wood.

## Example Kanji

### 林 (grove / woods)

Two trees standing together = a small forest or a quiet grove.

### 森 (forest)

Three trees gathered in one place = a deep, dense forest where the sunlight barely touches the ground.

### 本 (book / origin)

A tree with a single mark at its base (the roots) = the source, root, or origin of all things.
