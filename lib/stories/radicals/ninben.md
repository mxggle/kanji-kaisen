## Origin

The simplified form of 人 (person) when used as a radical on the left side. It shows a person viewed from the side, standing upright with their legs slightly apart.

## Story

See the person standing tall, ready to interact with the world! When this radical appears on the left, it signals that the kanji describes a human quality, a social relationship, or an action that people perform. It is the mark of our human presence in the written language.

## Position

Always appears on the LEFT side of the kanji

## Transforms Meaning

Represents human actions, social statuses, or basic human traits and states of being

## Example Kanji

### 休 (rest)

A person (亻) leaning against a tree (木) to take a cool, refreshing break from their work.

### 体 (body)

A person (亻) + foundation/origin (本) = the physical body is the foundation and origin of every living person.

### 信 (trust / faith)

A person (亻) + words (言) = a person standing firmly behind their words; the very heart of trust, belief, and honesty.
