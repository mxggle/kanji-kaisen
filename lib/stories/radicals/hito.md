## Origin

A pictograph showing a person in profile, standing with two legs. It represents the human being in its simplest form, emphasizing our upright stature and movement.

## Story

Two lines leaning on each other for support—that's 人! It shows how humans are not meant to stand alone; we lean on one another for strength and companionship. Whenever you see this, think of the vast tapestry of humanity and the individuals who make it up.

## Position

Appears as a standalone kanji, at the top (hitoyane), or becomes the LEFT radical (ninben)

## Transforms Meaning

Represents people, human qualities, interpersonal relationships, or the essence of being someone

## Example Kanji

### 大 (big)

A person (人) with arms spread wide as if saying "The fish I caught was this big!"

### 入 (enter)

A person (人) viewed as if they are stepping forward and crossing through a doorway into a room.

### 今 (now)

A person (the top roof-like part) gathering things in the present moment; the state of things right now.
