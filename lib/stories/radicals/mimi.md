## Origin

A detailed pictograph of a human ear, clearly showing the outer ridges of the ear (the left line) and the earlobe (the bottom).

## Story

Listen closely to the whispers of the world! This character traces the delicate shape of the human ear. It represents the sense of hearing and the deep act of listening—not just to sounds, but to the wisdom that the wind and others bring to us.

## Position

Standalone kanji or on the left side (mimihen)

## Transforms Meaning

Relates to sound, the act of hearing, obedience (listening to orders), or things that have ear-like handles or projections

## Example Kanji

### 聞 (hear / listen)

Gate (門) + ear = imagine putting your ear against a heavy wooden gate to hear the secrets being whispered on the other side.

### 取 (take / capture)

Ear + hand (又) = in ancient times, the 'ear' was taken as a trophy of capture. Now it means to take or obtain something with your hand.

### 聖 (holy / saint)

Ear + mouth + king (王 simplified) = a person who truly 'listens' to the divine and speaks with the wisdom of a great king.
