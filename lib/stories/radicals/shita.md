## Origin

The shita radical is a pictograph of a human tongue poking out of a mouth (口). The top strokes represent the shape of the tongue, and the bottom box is the mouth. It represents the physical organ of taste and the primary tool used for speaking.

## Story

Watch your language! This radical is the center of communication and flavor. Imagine a tongue moving skillfully to pronounce words or taste a savory meal. When you see it, think of the power of speech, the sharpness of a "tongue-lashing," and the intimate way we express our thoughts through sound.

## Position

Standalone kanji or appears on the left side of a character.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates the physical tongue, taste, speaking, words, communication, or actions involving the mouth and oral expression.

## Example Kanji

### 舌 (tongue)

The base kanji for the tongue, used in biological contexts and in metaphors about speaking and flavor.

### 話 (talk / story)

Words (言) + tongue (舌). The beautiful combination of "words" and the "tongue" moving together to tell a "story" or have a "talk."

### 辞 (word / resign / dictionary)

Tongue (舌 variant) + spicy/bitter. Originally related to an official "tongue" speaking bitter or formal words to quit a job or define a "word."
