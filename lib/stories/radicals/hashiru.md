## Origin

Combines the character for soil or earth (土) with a simplified foot (止). It depicts a person moving so quickly that their feet kick up dust from the ground.

## Story

Race across the fields! Imagine a person running with such speed and determination that clouds of dust rise from the earth (土) under their quick, pounding feet. It represents the thrill of the race, the urgency of speed, and any action that requires a swift burst of energy over the land.

## Position

Standalone kanji or on the left side (hashiruhen), wrapping slightly under the character

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates running, speed, racing, leaping, or any action that involves moving quickly from one point to another

## Example Kanji

### 起 (wake up / happen)

Run radical + snake/self (巳) = imagine a snake or a person suddenly 'running' up from the ground into an upright, active position.

### 越 (cross / exceed)

Run radical + battle-axe (戉) = running forward with a weapon in hand to cross a border or exceed a previously set limit.

### 超 (super / exceed)

Run radical + call/summon (召) = running with great speed after being summoned, leaping over obstacles to go far beyond the ordinary.
