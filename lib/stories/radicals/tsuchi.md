## Origin

A pictograph showing a mound of earth or the surface of the ground. The bottom horizontal line is the earth's surface, and the cross represents soil piled up or a sprout emerging.

## Story

Imagine a cross planted firmly in the ground—that's 土! It shows a stake or a seedling marking the earth, reminding us of farming, construction, and the solid foundation upon which we build our lives and cities.

## Position

Standalone kanji, on the left side (tsuchihen), or at the bottom

## Transforms Meaning

Relates to the ground, soil, specific locations, or the act of building and construction

## Example Kanji

### 地 (ground / earth)

Earth radical + also (也) = the actual physical ground we stand on and walk across.

### 場 (place / location)

Earth radical + change/spread (昜) = a designated piece of land where something specific happens.

### 坂 (slope / hill)

Earth radical + return/back (反) = land that rises up and forces you to work hard to climb—a slope.
