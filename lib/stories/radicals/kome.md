## Origin

The kome radical is a pictograph showing a rice plant with its grains scattering in all four directions from the central stalk. it captures the essence of the crop that has sustained East Asian civilizations for thousands of years.

## Story

Fuel for the soul! This radical represents the essential nourishment of the rice harvest and the intricate detail of individual grains. When you see it, think of the golden fields, the steam rising from a hot bowl of rice, and the tiny, powerful "seeds" that feed the world. It brings a sense of life-giving energy, organic detail, and cultural importance to every character it joins.

## Position

Often appears as a standalone kanji or at the bottom/left of complex characters.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates rice and cereal grains, food and nutrition, small particles (like powder or grains of sand), energy and fuel, or the concept of meticulous measurement.

## Example Kanji

### 米 (rice / grain / American)

The base kanji for the essential food crop, also used as a phonetically-selected symbol for America (the "Rice Country").

### 糖 (sugar / starch)

Rice (米) + court (唐). Originally referred to a specific type of sweet material derived from processed "grains" or starches—the modern word for sugar.

### 糞 (feces / excrement / dung)

Rice (米) + different/parts (異). A very direct representation of "rice" and other food that has been biologically "transformed" into a different state—representing excrement.
