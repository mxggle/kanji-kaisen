## Origin

The bokuzukuri radical is a pictograph of a hand (又) holding a stick or a tool, poised to strike or guide something. It is the active, forceful counterpart to the gentle hand radical, representing direct physical action.

## Story

Whack! A hand with a stick is ready to take command. This radical is all about force, guidance, and making things happen in the physical world. Whether it’s hitting a nail, folding a cloth, or guiding a student, it represents the active, "doing" part of a kanji character.

## Position

Always appears on the RIGHT side of the kanji.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates physical force, striking, guidance, transformation through action, or the process of making a change.

## Example Kanji

### 改 (reform / change / improve)

Self (己) + strike (攵). To "strike" one's own habits or old ways with a stick of discipline to "reform" and become better.

### 放 (release / set free / emit)

Direction (方) + strike (攵). Imagine striking an object in a specific "direction" to let it go or launch it into the world.

### 救 (rescue / save / help)

Seek (求) + strike (攵). To take decisive action (strike) in response to someone "seeking" help, pulling them out of danger.
