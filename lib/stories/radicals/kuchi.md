## Origin

A simple pictograph of an open mouth. Its square shape represents any kind of opening, entry, or the physical act of speaking and eating.

## Story

Open your mouth wide and what do you see? A simple, sturdy square! This character represents not just the part of our body that tastes and talks, but any entrance in the world—from a doorway to the mouth of a river. It is the gateway of communication and nourishment.

## Position

Standalone kanji, on the left side (kuchihen), or embedded within other characters

## Transforms Meaning

Relates to speech, eating, openings, or the number of people (mouths to feed) or items

## Example Kanji

### 言 (say / words)

Mouth + four lines showing sound waves emerging = the act of speaking words into the air.

### 品 (goods / quality)

Three mouths together = many items produced or goods to be consumed, eventually coming to represent quality.

### 問 (ask / question)

Gate (門) + mouth = imagine standing at a great gate and calling out a question to someone inside.
