## Origin

The hitsuji radical is a vivid pictograph of a sheep's head, clearly showing its curved horns and its muzzle. In ancient times, sheep were powerful symbols of beauty, goodness, and ritual sacrifice, representing prosperity and social harmony.

## Story

Gentle, pure, and good! This radical represents the positive traits traditionally associated with sheep—meekness, beauty, and the grace of a ritual offering. When you see it, think of moral goodness, aesthetic beauty, social harmony (like a peaceful flock), or objects and words that are pleasing to the soul. It brings a sense of sacrificial grace and communal peace to its characters.

## Position

Often appears as a standalone kanji or at the top of complex characters.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates goodness, beauty, righteousness, social groups/flocks, or concepts related to ritual sacrifice and peace.

## Example Kanji

### 美 (beautiful / good / sweet)

Sheep (羊) + big (大). In ancient times, a "big sheep" was the ultimate symbol of prosperity and "beauty"—it was the biggest and best thing one could own.

### 義 (justice / righteousness / meaning)

Sheep (羊) + I/my (我). Originally related to a "sheep" offered for "myself" in a solemn ritual, leading to the meaning of following what is moral and "just."

### 善 (good / virtuous / moral)

Sheep (羊) + words (言). Words that are as gentle and "good" as a sheep—representing the peak of moral virtue and social excellence.
