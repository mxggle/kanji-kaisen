## Origin

A detailed pictograph of a bird in profile. You can clearly see the head with its eye, the wings tucked in the middle, and the tail feathers fanning out at the bottom.

## Story

See the bird perched on a branch, ready to take flight! The top horizontal line is the head, the box with a dot is the sharp eye, and the strokes below show the beautiful feathers of the wings and tail. It capture the elegance and freedom of all winged creatures that soar through the sky.

## Position

Standalone kanji or typically on the right side of compounds

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates birds, flying creatures, the act of chirping or singing, or things related to flight

## Example Kanji

### 鳴 (chirp / cry out)

Mouth (口) + bird = the image of a bird opening its mouth wide to greet the morning sun with a song or a cry.

### 鳩 (pigeon / dove)

Bird + nine (九) = a clever character where 'nine' represents the cooing sound of a pigeon, which sounds like 'ku' in Japanese.

### 鶴 (crane)

Bird radical + a high, rising element = the elegant, long-necked bird that stands tall in the marshes and flies toward the heavens.
