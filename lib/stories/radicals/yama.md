## Origin

A clear pictograph of three mountain peaks rising against the sky. The central peak is the highest, flanked by two supporting ridges on either side.

## Story

Stand tall and look at the horizon! Three peaks reach for the clouds in perfect harmony. This character is the bedrock of geography, representing strength, height, and the enduring nature of the earth that outlasts human life.

## Position

Standalone, on the left (yamahen), or at the top (yamakanmuri)

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates mountains, rocks, height, or wild, natural places far from the city

## Example Kanji

### 岩 (rock / boulder)

Mountain + stone (石) = the large, heavy stones that make up the body of a mountain.

### 島 (island)

Mountain + bird (鳥 simplified) = a mountain rising out of the sea where weary birds go to rest.

### 岸 (shore / bank)

Mountain + cliff + dry = the high, dry land where the mountains meet the water's edge.
