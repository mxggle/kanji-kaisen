## Origin

A pictograph showing a mouth (口) at the bottom with several horizontal lines above representing the sound waves and vibration of the voice emerging into the air.

## Story

Words flowing from the heart and into the world! The lines radiating upward show the power of the voice as it leaves the mouth. This is the radical of language, literature, and the profound human act of telling our stories to one another through the ages.

## Position

Standalone kanji or on the left side (gonben 訁)

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates speaking, language, words, literature, or the act of communication and planning

## Example Kanji

### 話 (talk / story)

Speech radical + tongue (舌) = the sound of words rolling off the moving tongue as someone tells a vivid story.

### 論 (theory / logic)

Speech radical + organize/edit (侖) = words that have been carefully edited and organized into a sharp, logical argument or theory.

### 語 (language / word)

Speech radical + five + mouth = a complete language where many different 'mouths' speak in harmony with a shared understanding.
