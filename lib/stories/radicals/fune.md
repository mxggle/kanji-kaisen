## Origin

The fune radical is a pictograph of a small wooden boat or a traditional canoe, shown from above with its hollow hull and internal bracing. It captures the essential shape of a vessel designed to carry people and goods across the water.

## Story

Sail away! This radical represents the spirit of exploration and transport. Whether it’s a tiny rowboat on a quiet lake or a grand vessel crossing the ocean, "fune" brings the concept of the journey and the craftsmanship of navigation to any character. When you see it, think of the flowing water and the wooden structures that master it.

## Position

Often appears as a standalone kanji or on the left side (as "funehen") in complex characters.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates water transport, navigation, the shape of a vessel, or the act of carrying/transferring things across a distance.

## Example Kanji

### 舟 (boat / vessel)

The base kanji for a small boat, representing the simplest form of water transportation.

### 般 (sort / generally / kind)

Boat (舟) + action (殳). It originally referred to a boat being turned or maneuvered, eventually evolving to mean "a turn" or "a general type/sort."

### 航 (navigate / sail / fly)

Boat (舟) + stretch/high (亢). The action of a boat stretching its path across the wide ocean—navigating toward a distant shore.
