## Origin

Shows two tufts of grass or small plants sprouting from the ground. It is the simplified crown form of the kanji for grass (草).

## Story

Grass growing on top! Like a green crown upon the earth, this radical sits above kanji related to flowers, herbs, and vegetation. It’s the sign of life and growth, reminding us of the seasonal cycles of nature.

## Position

Always appears at the TOP of the kanji

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates plants, vegetation, herbs, flowers, or green growing things

## Example Kanji

### 花 (flower)

Grass radical + transform (化) = the part of a plant that transforms and blooms into beauty.

### 草 (grass)

Grass radical + early/sun (早) = the plants that are the first to grow in the early morning of spring.

### 茶 (tea)

Grass radical + person/roof + tree = a forest plant harvested by people to make a refreshing drink.
