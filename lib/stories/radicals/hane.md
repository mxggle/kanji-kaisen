## Origin

The hane radical is a pictograph of two bird feathers or a pair of wings. It captures the light, airy structure of the plumage used by creatures of the sky for flight, warmth, and protection.

## Story

Spread your wings and fly! This radical represents the lightness of birds, the incredible speed of flight, and the delicate beauty of a single feather. When you see it, think of soaring through the clouds, the soft touch of down, and the ultimate freedom of the sky. It brings a sense of movement, elevation, and effortless grace to its characters.

## Position

Usually appears at the top or as a standalone kanji.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates wings, feathers, flight, the concept of lightness, or actions that involve repetition and practice (like a bird learning to fly).

## Example Kanji

### 羽 (feather / wings)

The base kanji for a single feather or the majestic wings of a bird, representing the tool for flight.

### 習 (learn / practice / study)

Wings (羽) + white/sun (白). Imagine a young bird "practicing" its "wings" in the morning sun—the very definition of learning through steady repetition.

### 翌 (next / following)

Stand (立) + wings (羽). Originally related to a bird "standing" on its wings or poised to take flight on the "next" coming day.
