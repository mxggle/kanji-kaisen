## Origin

The mochiiru radical is a pictograph of a functional wooden bucket or a specifically designed tool. It depicts an object that has a clear, practical "use" or "utility" in the daily rhythm of human life and work.

## Story

Put it to good work! This radical represents the tools, methods, and procedures we use to solve problems and get things done efficiently. When you see it, think of high productivity, important business affairs, and the sheer practical utility of an object or an idea. It brings a sense of focused activity, functional purpose, and the industrious spirit of work to any character it joins.

## Position

Often appears as a standalone kanji or as a central component in complex characters.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates using an object or method, business affairs and tasks, utility and function, or things that are "ordinary" (having the same general use).

## Example Kanji

### 用 (use / utility / business / task)

The base kanji for using a physical tool or being actively involved in a specific professional task or "business" affair.

### 備 (provide / equip / prepare)

A person (人) + grass/herb (艹) + to use (用). To provide a "person" with all the essential "tools" and materials they can "use" to be fully and expertly prepared.

### 庸 (common / ordinary / mediocre)

Building/slant roof (广) + to use (用). Originally referred to something that had a regular, "building-wide" utility—representing the "common" or standard way of doing things.
