## Origin

The gatsu radical is a pictograph of a jagged, broken bone or a decomposing remains. it represents the inevitable end of life and the physical process of "death" and "decay" in the natural world.

## Story

The cycle turns! This radical represents the stark reality of mortality and the biological transformations that happen after life ends. When you see it, think of the fragile nature of our bodies, the solemnity of passing, and the deep, inescapable "decay" that eventually returns all things to the earth. it brings a sense of gravity, finality, and biological truth to any character it joins.

## Position

Often appears as a standalone component or as the main part of complex characters involving death.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates death and dying, decay and decomposition, broken bones or fragments, ruin and destruction, or the state of being unfortunate.

## Example Kanji

### 死 (death / die)

Bone (歹) + a person (ヒ/人). The literal and powerful representation of a "person" reaching the state of being a "bone"—the absolute definition of death.

### 殊 (special / particularly)

Bone (歹) + cinnabar/pill (朱). It originally referred to a "special" or distinctive way of "death"—now used to emphasize something that is particularly unique.

### 殉 (martyrdom / follow in death)

Bone (歹) + circle/period (旬). To follow someone into "death" as if completing a full "circle" of loyalty—the definition of a martyr.
