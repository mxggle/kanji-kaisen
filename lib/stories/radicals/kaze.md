## Origin

A complex character showing a gust of wind (the outer frame 几) and an insect (虫) inside. Ancient Chinese belief held that wind brought insects and diseases with it.

## Story

Imagine a powerful gust of wind blowing through a valley! The outer frame represents the forceful movement of the air. Inside is a small insect—reflecting the ancient belief that the wind was the breath of the world that carried life and tiny creatures wherever it blew.

## Position

Standalone kanji or an enclosing radical (kazegamae)

## Transforms Meaning

Relates to air, atmosphere, style, or things that move invisibly but powerfully through the world

## Example Kanji

### 嵐 (storm)

Mountain (山) + wind (風) = a fierce storm brewing high over the mountain peaks.

### 扇 (fan)

Door + wings/feathers = a small device that acts like a bird's wing to create a breeze in a room.

### 颯 (breeze / sudden)

Stand (立) + wind (風) = the feeling of standing tall as a brisk, sudden breeze brushes past you.
