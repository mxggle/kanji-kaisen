## Origin

A pictograph of a curved blade or knife. You can see the sharp cutting edge and the curve of the steel that allows it to slice through materials with ease.

## Story

Imagine the gleaming edge of a samurai's katana! This character captures the curve and the sharpness of a blade. It represents the power to cut, divide, and decide. When you see it, think of the precision and the strength required to separate one thing into many.

## Position

Standalone kanji, at the top, or becomes the RIGHT side radical (rittou)

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates swords, knives, the act of cutting, dividing, or the quality of being sharp and decisive

## Example Kanji

### 切 (cut / slice)

Sword radical + seven (phonetic/measure) = the act of using a sharp blade to slice through and measure out portions of something.

### 分 (divide / minute / part)

Sword radical + the number eight (八) = imagine a sword cutting an object into many separate pieces; to divide into parts.

### 初 (first / beginning)

Clothing (衣) + sword radical = the very first cut of the blade into cloth during the creation of a new garment.
