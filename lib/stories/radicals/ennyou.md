## Origin

A variant of the movement radical (辶), focusing specifically on the act of stretching or lengthening a stride. It depicts a leg reaching forward to cover a greater distance.

## Story

Step out with a long, confident stride! This radical stretches under the kanji like a person reaching out their leg to cover as much ground as possible. It carries the concept of extension, duration, and the deliberate stretching of time or space toward a far-off goal.

## Position

Always at the BOTTOM-LEFT, wrapping under the rest of the character

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates stretching, lengthening, extending a duration, or moving with long, formal strides

## Example Kanji

### 延 (extend / delay)

Stride radical + correct/stop (止 variant) = the act of stretching out the stopping point or the deadline; extending time or length.

### 廷 (court / palace)

Stride radical + stand tall (壬 form) = the long, formal hallways where people must 'stride' with dignity to stand before the emperor's throne.

### 建 (build / establish)

Stride radical + brush/writing (聿) = 'stretching' out the large-scale plans and 'writing' them into physical reality; the act of building a structure.
