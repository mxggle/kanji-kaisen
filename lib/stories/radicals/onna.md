## Origin

The onna radical is a pictograph of a woman kneeling or bowing in a traditional posture of respect or seated at a loom. The overlapping strokes represent the arms and legs in a graceful, settled position, capturing the historical role of women in the domestic and social sphere.

## Story

Grace and strength! This is the radical of the feminine, representing mothers, daughters, and the many roles women have played throughout history. When you see it, think of life, family, and the unique qualities of care and tradition. It is one of the most common radicals, appearing in hundreds of characters related to people.

## Position

Can appear as a standalone kanji, on the left side (onnahen), or at the bottom of a character.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates females, family relationships, emotional qualities, domestic actions, and traditionally feminine roles or names.

## Example Kanji

### 婦 (adult woman / wife)

Woman (女) + a broom (帚). Historically referred to a "woman" with a "broom," representing a wife or a mature woman in charge of the household.

### 娘 (daughter / young woman)

Woman (女) + good (良). A "good woman" of the family—the beloved "daughter" or a young girl full of potential.

### 始 (begin / start)

Woman (女) + pedestal/platform (台). Originally suggested the beginning of life through a "woman," eventually evolving to mean the start of any process or event.
