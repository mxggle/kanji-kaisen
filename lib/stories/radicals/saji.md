## Origin

The saji radical is a pictograph of a spoon or a ladle, specifically showing the curved bowl and the handle. In ancient times, it was also associated with the shape of a person sitting or a knife, leading to its frequent appearance in characters related to transformation and eating.

## Story

Scoop it up! Imagine a small, elegant spoon ready to serve a delicious meal. This radical represents the tools of nourishment and the act of change. When you see it, think of something being shifted, transformed, or scooped from one state to another. It's the radical of the "ladle."

## Position

Often appears on the right side or as a central component of a kanji.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates tools for eating (spoons), the act of transformation or change, or a seated posture.

## Example Kanji

### 化 (change into / transform)

Person (人) + spoon (匕). Originally depicted a person turning or flipping over (like a spoon being turned), representing the act of transformation or "becoming" something else.

### 北 (north)

Two people (匕 variant) back to back. While it looks like spoons, it represents two people turning away from each other to face the "cold" direction of the north.

### 匙 (spoon)

Correct/straight (是) + spoon (匕). The formal kanji for a "spoon," emphasizing the "correct" tool used for eating and serving food.
