## Origin

The sanbongawa radical is a pictograph of a flowing river, shown with three vertical (or slightly curved) strokes representing the ripples and currents of the water. "Sanbon" literally means "three pieces," referring to the three distinct lines that make up the character.

## Story

Go with the flow! Imagine the rushing water of a great river carving its path through the mountains. These three strokes represent the unstoppable power and grace of liquid in motion. When you see this radical, think of streams, valleys, and the journey of water as it travels toward the sea.

## Position

Standalone kanji or appears in the center or on the side of a character.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates rivers, flowing water, natural streams, valleys, or the concept of movement and traveling along a fixed path.

## Example Kanji

### 川 (river)

The base kanji for a river, capturing the essential image of flowing water that has shaped human civilization and the landscape.

### 州 (state / province)

River (川) + three dots (islands). Reprents "islands" of land settled within a great "river" system—the origin of the word for states and provinces.

### 巡 (patrol / go around)

Movement (辶) + river (巛 variant). To move along a path like a "flowing river," following a regular circuit or "patrolling" a specific area.
