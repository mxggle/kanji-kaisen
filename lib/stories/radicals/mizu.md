## Origin

A pictograph of a flowing stream. The central vertical stroke represents the main current, and the side strokes are the ripples and splashes of water as it moves.

## Story

See the river flowing before you! The strong central line is the deep, powerful current, while the smaller strokes on the sides are the splashes of water hitting rocks in the stream. Water is the essence of life, constantly changing and flowing through the world.

## Position

Standalone or usually at the bottom of the kanji

## Transforms Meaning

Relates to liquids, the act of flowing, wetness, or the concept of purification

## Example Kanji

### 氷 (ice)

Water + a drop (the dot) = a drop of water that has been 'stopped' or frozen solid by the cold.

### 泉 (spring / fountain)

White (白) + water = the pure, 'white' water bubbling up from a clear spring deep in the earth.

### 祭 (festival / ritual)

Meat (夕 form) + hand (又) + water (示 simplified) = the ritual of washing hands and offering food at a sacred festival.
