## Origin

The shinyou radical is a pictograph of a hand holding a branch or a stick. The top part is the branch, and the bottom "cross" (又) represents the hand gripping it. It captures the essence of using a branch for support or as a tool for measurement.

## Story

Lend a helping hand! Imagine holding a sturdy branch to steady yourself as you walk or to prop up a heavy structure. This radical is the symbol of support, delegation, and the many branches of a family or business. When you see it, think of a strong hand providing the stability needed to grow and expand.

## Position

Standalone kanji or appears on the right or bottom of a character.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates physical support, the branching nature of a tree or organization, financial payment (supporting a cost), or a helping action.

## Example Kanji

### 支 (branch / support)

The base kanji for support and branching, used in everything from "branch offices" to the act of "supporting" a friend.

### 枝 (branch / twig)

Tree (木) + branch/support (支). The physical "branch" that grows from the trunk of a "tree," providing the structure for leaves and fruit.

### 技 (skill / technique)

Hand (扌) + branch/support (支). The "support" and dexterity of a "hand" using tools or techniques—the essence of specialized "skill."
