## Origin

A pictograph of a small child or infant. The top portion (口) represents the head, the horizontal line shows the tiny arms reaching out, and the bottom suggests swaddling clothes or small legs.

## Story

See the baby reaching out from the cradle! This character captures the innocence and joy of youth. The top is the head, the arms are open for a hug, and the body is wrapped snugly in blankets. It represents new life, the next generation, and the potential of what is to come.

## Position

Standalone kanji, at the bottom, or on the left side (kohen)

## Transforms Meaning

Relates to children, youth, small things, or the 'offspring' or products of a process

## Example Kanji

### 学 (study / learn)

A child (子) under a roof (school) with hands above showing the passing down of knowledge and wisdom.

### 好 (like / good)

A woman (女) + a child (子) = the natural, purest form of affection and goodness found between a parent and their child.

### 字 (character / letter)

A child (子) under a roof (宀) = just as children are born and raised in a house, characters are 'born' and developed within the structures of the mind.
