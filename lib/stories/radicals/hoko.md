## Origin

The hoko radical is a pictograph of a long-handled spear or a halberd, featuring a pointed blade and a cross-piece at the top. It represents one of the primary weapons used on the ancient battlefields of East Asia.

## Story

Arm yourself! This radical carries the energy of the warrior and the sharp precision of a piercing blade. When you see it, think of weapons, protection, and the active struggle to overcome obstacles. It brings the power of determination and the strength to "pierce" through any challenge to the characters it joins. It is the radical of the defense and the decisive strike.

## Position

Often appears as a standalone kanji or on the left/top side of complex characters.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates weapons (spears, blades), protection, military force, the act of piercing or striking, or the concept of a "side" or "role" in a conflict.

## Example Kanji

### 矛 (spear / halberd)

The base kanji for a long-handled stabbing weapon, representing the fundamental tool of ancient combat.

### 柔 (tender / soft / flexible)

Spear (矛) + tree/wood (木). Imagine a spear handle made of high-quality "flexible" wood that can bend without breaking—the essence of true softness and strength combined.

### 務 (task / duty / business)

Spear (矛) + force (力) + task (攵). To wield a "spear" with "force" and directed "action"—the literal representation of taking on a solemn duty or a professional task.
