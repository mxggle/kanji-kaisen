## Origin

A simplified, vertical form of 犬 (inu - dog). While it comes from the symbol for dog, it has evolved to represent all four-legged mammals and wild beasts.

## Story

Watch the wild beast stalking through the brush! These three quick strokes capture the energy of a four-legged animal on the prowl. When you see it on the left side of a kanji, imagine the wild, untamed nature of cats, wolves, and other creatures of the wilderness.

## Position

Always appears on the LEFT side of the kanji

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates mammals, wild or domestic four-legged animals, or traits associated with bestial or wild behavior

## Example Kanji

### 猫 (cat)

Beast radical + seedlings (苗) = imagine a small, nimble beast moving stealthily through the green seedlings of a field.

### 独 (alone / single)

Beast radical + worm/insect (蜀) = can represent a single, lone animal hunting in the wild, signifying the state of being alone.

### 狂 (crazy / madness)

Beast radical + king (王) = imagine a wild beast that thinks it is a king, or a human king acting with the uncontrolled fury of a beast.
