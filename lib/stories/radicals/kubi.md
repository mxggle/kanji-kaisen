## Origin

A pictograph of a person's head. The top strokes represent hair or a crown, and the central part contains the eye (目). It represents the head and, by extension, the neck that supports it.

## Story

Look at the head of a great leader! The top strokes show hair standing tall, and the eye represents the vision and awareness of the person. It represents the neck that connects the mind to the body, and often symbolizes the 'head' or chief of an entire organization or path.

## Position

Standalone kanji or a components within compounds

## Transforms Meaning

Relates to the neck, the head, leadership, titles, or the beginning of something

## Example Kanji

### 道 (way / road)

Neck/head radical + movement (辶) = the path where your head points and your feet follow; the way of leading.

### 県 (prefecture / district)

Neck (inverted) + eye/small = in ancient times, related to governance and oversight of a specific geographic district.

### 題 (topic / title / problem)

Correct (是) + head (頁 simplified to 首) = the 'head' or main point that must be correctly understood or solved.
