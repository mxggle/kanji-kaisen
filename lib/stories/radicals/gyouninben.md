## Origin

A pictograph representing the left half of a crossroad (行). It shows one side of a path, indicating the rhythmic act of walking or moving along a street or thoroughfare.

## Story

Take the first step on your journey! These two quick strokes show the left side of a bustling street or a person taking a brisk step forward. It is the radical of public paths, travel, and the steady, rhythmic movements of people going about their daily lives.

## Position

Always appears on the LEFT side of the kanji

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates walking, streets, travel, social duties, or actions that take place on a path or road

## Example Kanji

### 行 (go / conduct)

Left step (彳) + right step (亍) = the complete, joined act of walking down a path; the base kanji for going or performing.

### 役 (duty / role)

Step radical + weapon (殳) = imagine a soldier marching down a road with their weapon, performing their necessary duty or role.

### 待 (wait)

Step radical + temple/measure (寺) = stopping your step at a specific place to 'measure' the time until your friend or a message finally arrives.
