## Origin

A pictograph of an ancient cart or chariot seen from above. The top and bottom horizontal lines show the wheels, and the central box is the axle and the body where goods or people are carried.

## Story

Look down on the world's first machines! This character shows the wheels and the frame of an ancient wagon. It represents the invention of the wheel and the power of transportation—carrying us and our inventions across vast distances with ease and speed.

## Position

Standalone kanji or on the left side (kurumahen)

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates vehicles, wheels, transportation, or anything that revolves and carries a load

## Example Kanji

### 転 (roll / turn)

Cart radical + cloud/revolve (云) = the image of wheels revolving and turning continuously as a cart travels down a long road.

### 軽 (light weight)

Cart radical + through/loom (圣) = an easy-to-handle cart that moves lightly and swiftly through the paths toward its destination.

### 輸 (transport / export)

Cart radical + gather/hollow (兪) = the act of gathering goods into wagons and carts to be sent out across the borders to other lands.
