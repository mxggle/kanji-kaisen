## Origin

A pictograph of a fish swimming in the water. The top (ク) is the head, the middle (田) represents the scales and body, and the four dots (灬) at the bottom are the tail fin.

## Story

Watch the fish dart through the clear stream! The top stroke is the pointed head, the central box shows the shimmering scales of its body, and the dots at the bottom are the fins propelling it through the water. It represents the bounty of the sea and the graceful life beneath the waves.

## Position

Standalone kanji or on the left side (uohen)

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates fish, seafood, aquatic life, or the act of fishing and preparing food from the sea

## Example Kanji

### 鮮 (fresh / vivid)

Fish radical + sheep (羊) = the two foods that ancient people valued most for being served perfectly 'fresh' and vibrant.

### 鯨 (whale)

Fish radical + capital/great (京) = the 'great fish' of the capital of the ocean; the massive whale that reigns over the deep.

### 鮭 (salmon)

Fish radical + soil/earth (圭) = the remarkable fish that returns across vast oceans to its original 'soil' (riverbed) to spawn.
