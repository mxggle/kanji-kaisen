## Origin

The tobu radical is an elaborate pictograph of a bird in flight. The central vertical part is the body, and the intricate strokes on the sides represent the outstretched wings and the ruffling of feathers as the bird soars through the air.

## Story

Take to the skies! Imagine a majestic bird spreading its wings and catching an updraft, rising higher and higher above the mountains. This radical is the ultimate symbol of flight, speed, and reaching great heights. When you see it, think of the freedom of the air, the roar of an airplane, and the amazing energy required to defy gravity.

## Position

Standalone kanji or appears as a primary component of a character.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates flying, rapid movement through the air, airplanes, high altitude, or the concept of things that "fly" or move with extreme speed.

## Example Kanji

### 飛 (fly)

The base kanji for flight, capturing the essential motion of birds and anything that travels through the sky.

### 飛行機 (airplane)

Fly (飛) + movement + machine. The "machine" that uses the power of "flight" and "movement" to travel across the world—a literal flying machine.

### 飛行場 (airport)

Fly (飛) + movement + place. The specialized "place" where birds and "flying" machines take off and land—a modern airport.
