## Origin

The mi radical is a pictograph of a standing person with a prominent, protruding stomach. it was originally a depiction of a pregnant woman, representing the physical "body" and the "self" as a complete, living vessel.

## Story

Listen to your body! This radical represents the physical vessel we inhabit and the core substance of our very being. When you see it, think of personal health, your unique physical form, and the "meat" or essential substance of a story or object. It brings a sense of grounding, physical presence, and the fundamental nature of the individual self to every character it joins.

## Position

Often appears as a standalone kanji or on the left side of complex characters.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates the physical body, personal status or social standing, health and physical condition, or the act of "shooting" out from the body (with the hand).

## Example Kanji

### 身 (body / self / status / social standing)

The base kanji for a person's physical "body" or their respected social "status" and "self" within the larger community.

### 射 (shoot / archery / field)

Body (身) + hand/inch (寸). Imagine a person's "body" and a steady "hand" working together in perfect harmony to release a powerful arrow—the physical act of shooting.

### 謝 (apologize / thank / gratitude)

Words (言) + shoot (射/身). It originally meant to "shoot" out sincere "words" to release a heavy burden of social obligation—by apologizing or saying thank you.
