## Origin

The amai radical depicts a mouth (口) with a single horizontal line inside it. This line represents a delicious morsel or a piece of candy being held on the tongue, capturing the literal sensation of savoring something sweet.

## Story

Mmm, something delicious is in the mouth! The little line nestled inside represents a sweet fruit or a treat being enjoyed. When this radical appears, it usually brings a sense of pleasure, indulgence, or a "sweet" quality to the character. Think of it as the literal taste of satisfaction.

## Position

Usually appears as the main component or at the bottom/center of a character.

## Transforms Meaning

Relates to sweetness, indulgence, pleasure, or the act of savoring something.

## Example Kanji

### 甘 (sweet)

The base kanji representing the taste of sugar or the quality of being indulgent (as in "spoiling" someone).

### 甚 (exceedingly / very)

Sweet (甘) + a tool/base (匹). It originally suggested an "excess" of sweetness or satisfaction, evolving to mean "extreme" or "very."

### 堪 (endure / bear)

Earth (土) + exceedingly/sweet (甚/甘). It refers to the ability to withstand extreme pressure or "swallow" a difficult situation, like enduring a bittersweet fate.
