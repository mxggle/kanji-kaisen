## Origin

A pictograph of a human foot at rest. The top strokes represent the toes pointing upward, and the bottom horizontal shows the heel planted firmly on the ground.

## Story

Stop right there! See the foot planted firmly on the earth. The character shows the toes pointing upward, indicating that the person has ceased their journey and is now standing perfectly still. It represents boundaries, the end of a path, and the moment of pausing.

## Position

Standalone kanji or positioned at the bottom or within other characters

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates stopping, staying, ceasing motion, or reaching a boundary or conclusion

## Example Kanji

### 歩 (walk / step)

Stop (止) + a simplified foot (少 form) = the rhythmic cycle of stepping forward and stopping; the physical act of walking.

### 正 (correct / right)

One (一) + stop = stopping exactly at the 'one' right place; the quality of being true, correct, and just.

### 歴 (history / curriculum)

Cliff/building (厂) + stop = the stopping points of time as they pass through the great events and structures of human history.
