## Origin

The mizugame radical is a pictograph of an earthenware jar featuring a sturdy, well-fitted lid. It represents various containers made of pottery or metal designed specifically to hold precious liquids or store essential household goods for long periods.

## Story

Fill it to the brim! This radical represents the durable, simple containers that have kept our water fresh and our food secure for countless generations. When you see it, think of heavy clay jars, modern metal cans, and the vital, everyday act of storage and preservation. It brings a sense of containment, protective boundaries, and the functional beauty of household vessels to any character it joins.

## Position

Often appears as a standalone kanji or on the left/right side of more complex characters.

## Transforms Meaning

Indicates pottery and clay vessels, modern cans and tins, the act of storage or containment, or things that are cracked and broken (like a dropped jar).

## Example Kanji

### 缶 (can / tin / jar)

The base kanji for a modern metal "can" or a traditional, heavy pottery "jar" used for the long-term storage of food or liquids.

### 罐 (can / jar / kettle)

Jar (缶) + stork (雚). A large, industrial-sized or specialized pottery "jar" or "can" designed for the storage and transport of larger quantities of goods.

### 罅 (crack / fissure)

Jar (缶) + shout/tiger marks (虖). Imagine a heavy pottery "jar" that has sustained a large, jagged "crack"—the literal definition of a physical fissure or gap.
